# A2 Spanish CEFR Level: Comprehensive Guidelines

## 1. Official CEFR Definition

According to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR), A2 level is defined as "Waystage" or "Elementary" proficiency.

### Core Competencies
An A2 level Spanish learner can:
- Understand sentences and frequently used expressions related to areas of immediate relevance (basic personal/family information, shopping, local geography, employment)
- Communicate in simple, routine tasks requiring simple and direct exchange of information
- Describe in simple terms aspects of their background, immediate environment, and matters of immediate need

**Official Source:** [Council of Europe CEFR](https://www.coe.int/en/web/common-european-framework-reference-languages)

## 2. Vocabulary Specifications

### Vocabulary Size
- **Range:** 1,000-2,000 words (receptive vocabulary)
- **Active vocabulary:** 800-1,500 words
- **Core functional vocabulary:** ~1,500 most frequent Spanish words

**Supporting Research:**
- Milton & Alexiou (2009): "Vocabulary size and the Common European Framework" - estimates A2 at 1,500-2,000 words
- Graded reader specifications indicate 600-800 unique words for A2 texts

### Thematic Vocabulary Areas
Essential topics covered at A2 level:
- Personal identification and family
- Home, accommodation, and surroundings
- Daily life and routines
- Free time and entertainment
- Travel and transportation
- Shopping and services
- Food and restaurants
- Health and body parts
- Weather and seasons
- Work and basic professions
- Education basics

## 3. Grammar Specifications

### Essential Grammar Structures

#### Verb Tenses and Moods
- **Present tense** (all regular and common irregular verbs)
- **Preterite** (Pretérito Indefinido) - completed past actions
- **Imperfect** (Pretérito Imperfecto) - descriptive past
- **Near future** (ir + a + infinitive)
- **Present progressive** (estar + gerundio)
- **Imperative** (informal commands - tú form)
- **Introduction to Present Perfect** (he/has + participio)

#### Grammatical Elements
- Quantifiers: mucho, poco, bastante, demasiado, suficiente, tanto
- Demonstratives: este, ese, aquel (all forms)
- Possessives: mi, tu, su, nuestro, vuestro (all forms)
- Direct object pronouns: lo, la, los, las
- Indirect object pronouns: me, te, le, nos, os, les
- Reflexive pronouns and verbs
- Comparatives: más...que, menos...que, tan...como
- Superlatives: el/la más + adjective
- Common prepositions: a, en, de, por, para, con, sin, desde, hasta
- Conjunctions: y, o, pero, porque, cuando, si
- Question words: qué, quién, cuándo, dónde, cómo, cuánto, por qué

#### Key Distinctions
- Ser vs. Estar (basic uses)
- Por vs. Para (basic distinctions)
- Saber vs. Conocer
- Indefinite vs. Definite articles

## 4. Sentence Structure and Complexity

### Optimal Sentence Characteristics
- **Average length:** 10-15 words
- **Maximum recommended:** 20 words
- **Minimum for substance:** 8 words

### Syntactic Features
- Predominantly simple sentences (subject + verb + object)
- Compound sentences with basic conjunctions (y, pero, o)
- Limited subordinate clauses
- Clear, linear word order (SVO predominant)
- Active voice preferred over passive
- Minimal embedding or recursion
- Direct, concrete language

### Readability Metrics
Spanish-specific readability formulas consider:
- Fernández-Huerta Index (adapted from Flesch for Spanish)
- Gutiérrez de Polini Formula (calibrated for Spanish texts)
- INFLESZ scale (Spanish readability standard)

## 5. Communication Functions

### Productive Skills (Speaking/Writing)
Learners can:
- Introduce themselves and others
- Describe family, living conditions, educational background
- Express likes, dislikes, and preferences
- Describe daily routines and habits
- Talk about past experiences (basic level)
- Make simple future plans
- Give and follow simple directions
- Make basic requests and offers
- Express agreement and disagreement
- Write short personal messages, emails, postcards
- Fill in forms with personal details

### Receptive Skills (Listening/Reading)
Learners can understand:
- Short, clear, simple messages and announcements
- Basic personal and family information
- Shopping situations and prices
- Simple directions and instructions
- Main points in short news items (with support)
- Simple personal letters and emails
- Basic signs, notices, and posters

## 6. DELE A2 Exam Specifications

### Exam Structure
- **Reading:** 60 minutes, 4 tasks, 25 questions
- **Writing:** 50 minutes, 2 tasks
- **Listening:** 35 minutes, 5 tasks
- **Speaking:** 12 minutes (+ 12 preparation), 3 tasks

### Passing Requirements
- Minimum 60% overall (60/100 points)
- Minimum 30 points per skill group:
  - Group 1: Reading + Writing (30/50 minimum)
  - Group 2: Listening + Speaking (30/50 minimum)

## 7. Text Characteristics

### Discourse Features
- Clear, logical organization
- Explicit connections between ideas
- Chronological or simple thematic ordering
- High frequency of concrete nouns vs. abstract concepts
- Limited use of figurative language
- Repetition of key vocabulary for reinforcement

### Typical Text Types
- Personal letters and emails
- Simple narratives and descriptions
- Basic instructions and recipes
- Short news items (simplified)
- Advertisements and notices
- Simple biographies
- Tourist information
- Weather reports

## 10. Quality Indicators

### Well-Formed A2 Text Should Have:
- 80-85% vocabulary within the 1,500 most frequent words
- Average sentence length 10-15 words
- Simple past and present tenses dominating (70%+ of verbs)
- Clear paragraph structure (3-5 sentences per paragraph)
- Concrete rather than abstract vocabulary (80%+ concrete nouns)
- High cohesion through repetition and simple connectors
- Limited idiomatic expressions (less than 5% of content)

### Red Flags for Exceeding A2:
- Subjunctive mood usage (except fixed expressions)
- Complex conditional sentences
- Literary or journalistic style markers
- Abstract philosophical concepts
- Technical jargon without support
- Sentences exceeding 25 words
- Multiple embedded clauses
- Extensive use of passive voice
